Key Issues

Aqua Culture

Aquaculture can have significant environmental impacts, such as water pollution, habitat destruction, and the spread of diseases and parasites to wild fish populations.

Saving the forest

Forests are home to a diverse range of plant and animal species, many of which are endemic and endangered. Deforestation disrupts these habitats, leading to the loss of biodiversity and the potential extinction of numerous species.

Global warming

Rising temperatures intensify heatwaves, droughts, and extreme weather events like hurricanes and cyclones. These events disrupt ecosystems, damage infrastructure, and pose significant risks to human health and well-being.

Biodiversity

One of the most significant challenges to biodiversity is the loss and fragmentation of natural habitats. Human activities, such as deforestation, urbanization, and conversion of land for agriculture, disrupt and destroy habitats, leading to the displacement and extinction of numerous species.

Chemical sprays

They may contaminate soil, water bodies, and air, leading to ecological imbalances and impacting non-target organisms, including beneficial insects, birds, and aquatic life. Prolonged use can disrupt ecosystems, reduce biodiversity, and harm natural habitats.
